Prediction visualization adapted from the presentation.

Overview

Building Energy Management Systems (BEMS) require reliable load prediction to operate efficiently.

This project develops a forecasting pipeline and evaluates how well ML models capture hourly and daily demand patterns. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Dataset & Key Observation

Hourly dataset includes electricity demand, weather (e.g., T2M, QV2M, TQL, W2M), and calendar indicators. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Finding: Temperature shows a positive trend but is not fully linear; it explains less than half of the variance (R² ≈ 0.42). :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Why Temperature Alone Is Not Enough

Cooling demand (summer) and heating demand (winter) both raise electricity load, creating season-dependent relationships.

Seasonal R² varies (about 0.39–0.54), so temperature is not a stable single predictor. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Method

Pipeline: Data Loading → Preprocessing → Feature Engineering → Model Training → Testing → Visualization. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Time-based features (e.g., lag signals) help capture daily/weekly structure, improving performance beyond weather-only baselines.

Results

Random Forest achieves strong accuracy (R² ≈ 0.986). :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Strength: Tracks stable periods well; daily and weekly cycles are captured effectively. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Limitation: Errors increase during volatile spikes; long-term dependencies may need deep models. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
